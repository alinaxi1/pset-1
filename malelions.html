Male Lions

Male lions are the protectors of the pride. They regularly patrol the pride territory to fend off rival males and other threats to the pride. Male lions are born without manes. During adolescence, the mane is blond and scruffy. As the lion matures, the mane darkens and fills out. Rival males can challenge each other for territory and control of the pride. The lions fight and the winner chases the loser off. These contests are very dangerous. Participants are often wounded and could even be killed. When a new male takes over, he will most likely kill the old ruler's cubs.

Coalitions

Prides usually only have one male lion. If there are multiple males in a pride, they are most likely brothers. A team of brothers are known as a coalition. The rest of the pride consists of lionesses and cubs. Only the current ruler(s) of the pride can father cubs.
